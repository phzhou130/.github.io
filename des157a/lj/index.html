Journal One

Oct/15th

After reading Best practices for form design, I learned the importance of creating a connection between the users and the product. For example, the website needs to attract users through using psychological skills, such as letting them feel safe when they need to provide some private information and making users spend less time during the input process. I can relate with these suggestions. While purchasing stuff online, I will feel impatient if the website asks me to register an account before checking out. During the creation of an account, I will reconsider whether I need to buy the stuff or not. Sometimes, I would end up realizing that the items in my cart are not necessary and won’t purchase them. If the website doesn't have the account creation process during the check out step, I will buy the items without consideration.

As a designer, combining this story and the articles’ ideas makes me realize that the psychological path of users can not be ignored. Other than this experience, the article’s point about protecting users’ privacy brought out another memory from my previous internship experience. At that time, I was working in a Robotic company which sells floor washing robots. The marketing team hoped to get some feedbacks from their users. However, less than 10% of the users were willing to let the company researchers visit their home. Also, the address they put did not include their door number, listing only the office location of their community. This situation changed when the website showed that users could get free gift if they provide their accurate addresses—including their door number. This story reflected the idea of making users feel safe about providing their information in the article. Therefore, after reading the article, the psychological skills it provided gave me a strong impression.

This requires users to create an account before shopping, stopping my roommate to register an account.

The article gives me concepts about how to illustrate my story by using images. I agree with a lot the points inside but I also hold opinions about some of the aspects talked in this article. I do think practicing regularly would help with telling stories by images. While I was hanging out with my friends, I always randomly took pictures to record what was happening. On Monday after class, when I was going through pictures I took, I really enjoy the memories brought up by these pictures. Also, I could feel that these images could inspires viewer to communicate with each other about the contents. The part 8 of this article, it mentions about the photographer could use photographs to inspires audiences sharing their points. I think this is one of the most important part of pictures—recording what is happening and bringing up different views.

While I was reading the article, the image about asking open-ended questions attracted me a lot. I noticed one thing that I want to reflect when I was taking pictures—show respects to the people who present themselves in the picture and show respect to the content. When I was watching documentaries or pictures that describing developing countries on social media, I realized that a lot of photographers are placing themselves in a higher position compared to the people in the photos. Talking about open-ended questions, the photo itself shouldn't led an aspect to viewers. I hope when I was taking photos, I could deliver the pictures to audience without add my views in the picture.
